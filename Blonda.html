About the Commodity

GENERAL

• Lac is a natural resin which is the only known commercial resin of animal origin. It is the hardened secretion of a tiny insect, Laccifer lacca (Kerr) popularly known as the Lac Insect.
• It is being cultivated and processed in the tropical regions of the India in the states of Jharkhand, Chattisgarh and parts of Madhya Pradesh and West Bengal.
• The region of Jharkhand and West Bengal produce the best quality of lac (Kusmi strain) in the World and the Kusmi Strain is popularly known for its colour and glossiness.
• It is 100% Natural, Organic and has GRAS status by the Food and Drug Administration (FDA) in US which means that it is generally recognized as safe in foods. It is also approved for use in products certified as organic by The United States Department of Agriculture (USDA).

TECHNICALS

• It is associated with an odoriferous compound, a wax and a mixture of dyes such as Erythrolaccin and Desoxyerythrolaccin which are Hydroxyanthraquinone derivatives.
• It is insoluble in water, glycerol, hydrocarbon solvents and esters but dissolves readily in alcohol, aqueous solution of alkalies, organic acids and ketones.

Application

Lac being 100% Natural, Organic and because of its outstanding properties and remarkable versatility, it has been and is being put into innumerable applications across multiple industries. It’s applications in some major industrial and domestic fields are –

As a Film former / Organic Coating formulation

French Polishes Coating over Chocolates and Coffee beans
Metal Foil Pharmaceuticals – Capsules coating
Glaze for Confectionery Aqueous varnishes for leather dressing
Coating over Fruits – Apple, Kinnow Paper Varnishes
Enamels & Wood Sealers Undercoats

As a Plastic

In grinding wheels General Moulded articles
Sealing Waxes

As an Insulator

Insulating varnishes Micanites and Micafolium
Laminated paper products Insulating cloth

As an Adhesive and Cement

Plate sealer Abrasive paper and cloth
Gasket cement Capping cement for electrical lamps and radio valves
General cement Optical Cement

In Miscellaneous Applications

Lithographic, Waterproof and Coloured ink Dental Compositions
Photographic and engraving industry Fireworks
Rubber goods Shoe Industry Shoe Industry
Hat proofing Playing card finish
Cosmetics – nail polishes Oil varnish
Lacquerware and Toys Anti-fouling paints
Etch primer Bangle Making
